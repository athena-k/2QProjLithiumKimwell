Whether you're preparing sweets, soups, or anything in between, you'll be sure to notice references to Japanese cooking!

What's unique about it?
There are 33 local dishes. Like Liyue, a lot of its dishes (around a third of them) include rice in the recipe. There's a lot of seafood used too, such as shrimps, fish, and unagi (Japanese eels), which is a reference to the vast seas separating the region's islands; they must surely use their natural oceans as another food source! Onigiri, Tonkotsu Ramen, and Sakura Tempura are some typical Inazuma dishes!

Local Specialties
Inazuma actually has 9 local specialties, but only 1 can be used as an ingredient. This is the Sakura Bloom, a petal fallen from the grand shrine's sacred sakura. It adds a distinctly pink color to its dishes, making them look more aesthetically pleasing.

(Source of Picture: Genshin Impact Wiki - Sakura Bloom)
Characters
Like in the previous regions, Inazuman playable characters also have their Specialty Dishes. A good example is Kamisato Ayaka, whose specialty dish is "Snow on the Hearth", derived from the Inazuman dish, Sakura Mochi. Unlike most others, we actually know the reason why in-story; when we go to a festival with her, she likes the mochi and asks for the recipe, wanting to try making it herself.

So far, only one Inazuman character, Raiden, has notable cooking skills, but for a different reason. She's the only character so far who can't cook at all! Moving on from that, some well-known NPC cooks in Inazuma are Kiminami Anna (who works at Kiminami Restaurant), and Shimura Kanbei (who owns Shimura's). There's also a quest in Inazuma about a group of people (literally called Gourmet Supremos) who are working to find an ultimate recipe.
